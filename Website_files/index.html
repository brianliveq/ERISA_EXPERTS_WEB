Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an independent fiduciary under ERISA?

An independent fiduciary is a firm or individual that formally accepts fiduciary status in writing under ERISA §3(21) or §3(38), assuming legal responsibility for retirement plan decisions — not merely providing advice. Unlike a typical advisor, an independent fiduciary is legally accountable for the decisions made on your plan.

An independent fiduciary is one who is impartial and separate from the plan sponsor and its administrators. In the case of ERISA Experts, we are not an ERISA Section 3(21) or Section 3(38) fiduciary, but we do accept legal responsibility for the administration and management of an employee benefit plan. ERISA Experts acts in the sole interest of the plan and its participants.

How does hiring ERISA Experts as an independent fiduciary reduce plan sponsor liability?

When the plan sponsor hires ERISA Experts as an Independent Fiduciary, the plan sponsor delegates difficult decision-making powers to ERISA Experts. Doing so reduces personal liability exposure for company executives, HR leaders, and employee benefit committees. Note that hiring an independent fiduciary is itself an ERISA fiduciary action that remains with such executives, et al.

What does ERISA Section 404 require of plan sponsors?

ERISA Section 404 requires plan fiduciaries to act prudently and solely in the interest of plan participants and beneficiaries. This includes selecting and monitoring investments, ensuring reasonable fees, and following the plan document. Failure to meet these standards can result in personal liability for plan losses. Company officials can transfer almost all these responsibilities to an Independent Fiduciary.

How do I know if my current consultant is truly an ERISA fiduciary?

Ask your consultant to provide written confirmation of its ERISA fiduciary status and whether the consultant formally accepts liability for advice given. Many consultants use the word "fiduciary" in marketing materials without formally accepting ERISA fiduciary status. A true fiduciary confirms that legal status as part of its engagement.

What triggers an ERISA lawsuit against a plan sponsor?

Common triggers include excessive plan fees, imprudent investment selection, failure to monitor plan investments, lack of documentation of fiduciary decisions, and conflicts of interest. ERISA class action lawsuits have increased significantly in recent years, targeting both large and mid-size plan sponsors.

How much does hiring ERISA Experts as an Independent Fiduciary cost?

The scope of the engagement, the complexity of the plan, the level of liability assumed, and whether the work is ongoing or tied to a specific transaction all determine the cost. In most cases, ERISA Experts charges a flat fee or project fee rather than a one-size-fits-all price.

 

For limited scope assignments, fees may start in the several-thousand-dollar range. For broader ongoing engagements involving meaningful fiduciary oversight, governance support, conflict management, or assumption of named fiduciary responsibility, ERISA Experts typically charges higher fees and provides custom quotes. Evaluate the fee not by looking for the cheapest option, but by assessing whether the compensation is reasonable in light of the risk ERISA Experts assumes, the expertise involved, and the protection ERISA Experts provides to the plan sponsor.
